To search for specific leads, simply type the lead's X/Twitter name into the search bar.

<video autoplay="true" muted="true" loop playsinline className="w-full aspect-video" src="/videos/guides_crm_filter.mp4" />
You can also leverage the filter function to narrow down your leads. Navigate to the top right corner of the CRM to see the filtering options. Here's what each filter means:
<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Current Label">Selecting a label here will display only these labels. Other labels will be hidden.</Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Lists">If you've created a list, you can filter to only show leads that are members of specific lists. Choosing multiple lists will show leads that are in either list.</Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Value">Filter leads based on their assigned lead value. You can select leads whose value is above or below a certain value. Click the comparison criteria to change the type of filter.</Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Opportunity Score">Filter leads based on their opportunity score. You can select leads whose opportunity score is above or below a certain value. Click the comparison criteria to change the type of filter.</Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Contact Details">Filter leads based on available contact details such as name, email, or website. You can select multiple contact details, and it will show leads who have **all** selected contact details filled in.</Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Last Interaction">Choose from past 24 hours, past 7 days, or past 30 days to filter conversations based on the time of the last interaction.</Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Date Added">Choose from past 24 hours, past 7 days, or past 30 days to filter conversations based on when they were added to your CRM.</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
